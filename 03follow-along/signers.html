Who Were the Signers

These individuals stood out not just for signing the Declaration but for their larger roles in shaping the birth and early governance of the United States.

Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson, a delegate from Virginia, was the principal author of the Declaration of Independence. He later served as the 3rd President of the United States and is remembered for his contributions to American ideals of liberty and democracy.
John Adams
John Adams, representing Massachusetts, was a vocal advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. He became the 2nd President of the United States and played a key role in drafting foundational documents and promoting the cause of freedom.
Benjamin Franklin
Benjamin Franklin, from Pennsylvania, was a distinguished diplomat, inventor, and writer. He helped secure critical French support during the American Revolution and remains a symbol of American ingenuity and statesmanship.
John Hancock
John Hancock, a delegate from Massachusetts, was the first to sign the Declaration of Independence and is famous for his bold and prominent signature. As President of the Second Continental Congress, he was a key leader in the move toward independence.
Samuel Adams
Samuel Adams, also from Massachusetts, was a leader in early resistance to British policies and helped organize the Boston Tea Party. Known as the "Father of the American Revolution," he played a pivotal role in rallying colonists to the cause of liberty.
